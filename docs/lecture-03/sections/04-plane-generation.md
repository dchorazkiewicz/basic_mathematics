## Planes in three-dimensional space {#plane-generation}

A line was produced by allowing one real parameter,

$$
P=P_0+t\mathbf v.
$$

Only one independent direction of motion was available.

> **Guiding question.** What set is obtained when motion from one fixed point is allowed in two independent directions?

Let $P_0\in\mathbb R^3$ be fixed and let $\mathbf u,\mathbf v$ be nonparallel vectors.

!!! derivation "Generating the plane"
    Every permitted displacement is a linear combination $s\mathbf u+t\mathbf v$. Starting at $P_0$ gives

    $$
    P=P_0+s\mathbf u+t\mathbf v.
    $$

    One parameter produces a line; two independent parameters produce a plane. If the directions were parallel, both parameters would still move only along one line.

!!! definition "Parametric plane"
    $$
    \boxed{\Pi=\{P_0+s\mathbf u+t\mathbf v:s,t\in\mathbb R\}}.
    $$

    If $P_0=(x_0,y_0,z_0)$, $\mathbf u=[u_1,u_2,u_3]$, and $\mathbf v=[v_1,v_2,v_3]$, then

    $$
    \boxed{
    \begin{cases}
    x=x_0+su_1+tv_1,\\
    y=y_0+su_2+tv_2,\\
    z=z_0+su_3+tv_3.
    \end{cases}}
    $$
