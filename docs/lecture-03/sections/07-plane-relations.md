  <h2>Relative positions of planes</h2>
  <p>Let</p><div class="display-math">$$\Pi_1:\mathbf n_1\cdot P=d_1,\qquad \Pi_2:\mathbf n_2\cdot P=d_2.$$</div>
  <div class="statement"><div class="statement-label">Normals control relative position</div><p>If the normals are parallel, the planes have the same orientation and are either identical or distinct and parallel. If the normals are not parallel, the planes intersect in a line.</p></div>
  <div class="statement"><div class="statement-label">Angle and intersection direction</div><div class="display-math">$$\boxed{\Pi_1\perp\Pi_2\iff\mathbf n_1\cdot\mathbf n_2=0},$$</div><div class="display-math">$$\boxed{\mathbf v=\mathbf n_1\times\mathbf n_2}$$</div><p>is a direction vector of the intersection line when the planes are nonparallel.</p></div>
  <p>The cross product gives the direction because the intersection line must be perpendicular to both normals. A point on the line is then found by solving the two plane equations simultaneously.</p>

  <h2>Distance from a point to a plane</h2>
  <div class="derivation"><h3>Distance as a normal projection</h3><p>Let $P_0$ lie on the plane. The shortest displacement from $P$ follows the normal direction, so</p><div class="display-math">$$d(P,\Pi)=\frac{|\mathbf n\cdot(P-P_0)|}{\|\mathbf n\|}. $$</div></div>
  <div class="statement"><div class="statement-label">Point-to-plane distance</div><p>For $ax+by+cz=d$ and $P=(x_P,y_P,z_P)$,</p><div class="display-math">$$\boxed{d(P,\Pi)=\frac{|ax_P+by_P+cz_P-d|}{\sqrt{a^2+b^2+c^2}}}. $$</div><p>The numerator is signed normal displacement; the denominator converts the chosen normal to unit length.</p></div>
  <div class="statement"><div class="statement-label">Line and plane from permitted directions</div><p>A line is generated by one independent direction, $P=P_0+t\mathbf v$, whereas a plane is generated by two, $P=P_0+s\mathbf u+t\mathbf v$. The cross product produces a normal, and the dot product turns perpendicularity into the equation $\mathbf n\cdot(P-P_0)=0$.</p></div>
