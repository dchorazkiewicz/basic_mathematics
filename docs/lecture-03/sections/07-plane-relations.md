## Relative positions of planes {#plane-relations}

Let $P_1$ and $P_2$ be fixed points on two planes and let $\mathbf n_1,\mathbf n_2$ be their nonzero normal vectors. Write

$$
\Pi_1=\{P:\mathbf n_1\cdot[P-P_1]=0\},
\qquad
\Pi_2=\{P:\mathbf n_2\cdot[P-P_2]=0\}.
$$

Here $[P-P_i]$ is a free displacement and each dot product is a real number.

!!! principle "Normals control relative position"
    If the normals are parallel, the planes have the same orientation and are either identical or distinct and parallel. If the normals are not parallel, the planes intersect in a line.

!!! theorem "Angle and intersection direction"
    $$
    \boxed{\Pi_1\perp\Pi_2\iff\mathbf n_1\cdot\mathbf n_2=0},
    $$

    and, when the planes are nonparallel,

    $$
    \boxed{\mathbf v=\mathbf n_1\times\mathbf n_2}
    $$

    is a free direction vector of their intersection line.

The cross product gives the direction because the intersection line must be perpendicular to both normals. A point $P_0$ on the line is found by solving both scalar plane equations simultaneously. The intersection line is then

$$
\boxed{
L=\{P_0\oplus t(\mathbf n_1\times\mathbf n_2):t\in\mathbb R\}.
}
$$

## Distance from a point to a plane

!!! derivation "Distance as a normal projection"
    Let $P_0$ lie on the plane and let $P$ be any point. The free displacement from $P_0$ to $P$ is $[P-P_0]$. Its scalar component in the normal direction is

    $$
    \frac{\mathbf n\cdot[P-P_0]}{\|\mathbf n\|}.
    $$

    Therefore

    $$
    \boxed{
    d(P,\Pi)=\frac{|\mathbf n\cdot[P-P_0]|}{\|\mathbf n\|}.
    }
    $$

!!! theorem "Point-to-plane distance"
    For $ax+by+cz=d$ and $P=(x_P,y_P,z_P)$,

    $$
    \boxed{d(P,\Pi)=\frac{|ax_P+by_P+cz_P-d|}{\sqrt{a^2+b^2+c^2}}}.
    $$

    The numerator is signed normal displacement; the denominator converts the chosen normal to unit length.

!!! summary "Line and plane from permitted directions"
    A line is generated by one independent free direction,

    $$
    P=P_0\oplus t\mathbf v,
    $$

    whereas a plane is generated by two,

    $$
    P=P_0\oplus(s\mathbf u+t\mathbf v).
    $$

    The cross product produces a normal, and the dot product turns perpendicularity into the scalar equation

    $$
    \mathbf n\cdot[P-P_0]=0.
    $$
