  <h3>A. Elementary computational and graphical exercises</h3>
  <ol class="exercise-list">
    <li>Let $A=(1,2)$ and $B=(5,-1)$. Compute $[\overrightarrow{AB}]$. Draw equal representatives beginning at $O=(0,0)$ and $P=(-2,3)$, and find their endpoints.</li>
    <li>For $\mathbf u=[3,-2]$ and $\mathbf v=[-1,4]$, compute $\mathbf u+\mathbf v$, $\mathbf u-\mathbf v$, $2\mathbf u$, and $-3\mathbf v$. Draw the parallelogram for $\mathbf u+\mathbf v$.</li>
    <li>For $\mathbf u=[1,2]$ and $\mathbf v=[2,-1]$, compute $2\mathbf u-3\mathbf v$ and reproduce it head to tail.</li>
    <li>Compute the lengths and unit vectors for $[3,4]$, $[-5,12]$, and $[2,-2]$.</li>
    <li>Classify the pairs $[2,1],[6,3]$; $[2,1],[1,-2]$; $[3,-1],[2,5]$ as parallel, perpendicular, or neither.</li>
    <li>Find the angles between $[1,0]$ and $[1,1]$, and between $[1,1]$ and $[-1,1]$.</li>
    <li>For $\mathbf u=[3,1]$ and $\mathbf v=[-1,2]$, compute $\|\mathbf u-\mathbf v\|$ directly and from the cosine theorem.</li>
    <li>Project $[4,3]$ onto $[1,0]$ and onto $[1,1]$. Verify that each remainder is perpendicular to the target direction.</li>
    <li>For $\mathbf b_1=[1,1]$, $\mathbf b_2=[1,-1]$, and $\mathbf u=[4,2]$, find $[\mathbf u]_B$ and reconstruct $\mathbf u$.</li>
    <li>Starting from $P=(2,-1)$, apply $[3,2]$, $[-1,4]$, and $-2[3,2]$. Find the endpoint in two ways.</li>
  </ol>

  <h3>B. Development and reasoning exercises</h3>
  <ol class="exercise-list" start="11">
    <li>Explain with a parallelogram why translating an arrow without changing its directed change does not change the free vector. Give a numerical example.</li>
    <li>Draw $(\mathbf u+\mathbf v)+\mathbf w$ and $\mathbf u+(\mathbf v+\mathbf w)$ for concrete vectors and explain geometrically why the endpoints coincide.</li>
    <li>Prove that two nonzero plane vectors are parallel exactly when one is a scalar multiple of the other. Discuss positive and negative multiples separately.</li>
    <li>Find equal-length vectors with $\|\mathbf u+\mathbf v\|<\|\mathbf u\|$ and explain the cancellation geometrically.</li>
    <li>Derive $\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=\|\mathbf u\|\|\mathbf v\|\cos\theta$ from the cosine theorem and interpret the sign of the dot product.</li>
    <li>Prove from the projection formula that $\mathbf u-\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf v$.</li>
    <li>Explain why two nonparallel basis vectors give unique coefficients in $\mathbf u=\alpha\mathbf b_1+\beta\mathbf b_2$.</li>
    <li>Explain the type difference between $P=(3,2)$ and $\mathbf v=[3,2]$ using one location example and one displacement example.</li>
    <li>Find the standard and $B$-coordinates of $[6,2]$ for $\mathbf b_1=[1,1]$, $\mathbf b_2=[1,-1]$. Explain when a basis is well adapted.</li>
    <li>Refute the claim that every pair of nonzero vectors is a basis. Give one dependent and one independent example and interpret their linear combinations.</li>
  </ol>
