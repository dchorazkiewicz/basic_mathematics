Equations as geometric descriptions

An equation does not merely produce a number. It selects points whose coordinates satisfy a common condition. Changing a parameter changes the geometric set continuously.

The same algebraic language describes qualitatively different sets: a boundary, a filled region, an unbounded line or a curved graph. Parameters control position, scale and shape.

Intersection principle

A point lying on two curves satisfies both equations simultaneously. For example, intersections of $y=x^2$ and $y=x+2$ are exactly the solutions of $x^2=x+2$.
