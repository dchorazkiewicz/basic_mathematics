How much motion and in which direction?

The coordinates $[v_1,v_2]$ record horizontal and vertical change. A vector also carries two more geometric pieces of information: its magnitude and its direction.

Length from the Pythagorean theorem

The horizontal and vertical components form the legs of a right triangle, while the vector is its hypotenuse:

$$\|\mathbf v\|^2=v_1^2+v_2^2.$$

The norm is nonnegative, hence

$$\boxed{\|\mathbf v\|=\sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2}.}$$

For points $A$ and $B$, the displacement and distance are related by

$$\boxed{d_E(A,B)=\|B-A\|.}$$

Separating magnitude from direction

For a nonzero vector with inclination angle $\alpha$ measured from the positive $x$-axis,

$$\cos\alpha=\frac{v_1}{\|\mathbf v\|},\qquad \sin\alpha=\frac{v_2}{\|\mathbf v\|}.$$

Therefore

$$\boxed{\mathbf v=\|\mathbf v\|[\cos\alpha,\sin\alpha].}$$
Interpretation

$\|\mathbf v\|$ says how much displacement occurs. The unit vector $[\cos\alpha,\sin\alpha]$ says in which direction it occurs.
