Addition, opposite vectors, and subtraction

Let $\mathbf a=[a_1,a_2]$ and $\mathbf b=[b_1,b_2]$. Choose representatives head to tail: $\overrightarrow{PQ}$ represents $\mathbf a$ and $\overrightarrow{QR}$ represents $\mathbf b$. The arrow $\overrightarrow{PR}$ records the combined change.

Why the coordinates add

Since $Q=P+(a_1,a_2)$ and $R=Q+(b_1,b_2)$,

$$R=P+(a_1+b_1,\,a_2+b_2).$$

Therefore the combined displacement depends only on $\mathbf a$ and $\mathbf b$:

$$\boxed{\mathbf a+\mathbf b=[a_1+b_1,\,a_2+b_2].}$$

The parallelogram construction shows geometrically that the order does not matter:

$$\boxed{\mathbf a+\mathbf b=\mathbf b+\mathbf a.}$$

The opposite vector has the same length and direction line but reversed orientation:

$$-\mathbf a=[-a_1,-a_2],\qquad \mathbf a+(-\mathbf a)=\mathbf0.$$

Subtraction is addition of the opposite:

$$\boxed{\mathbf a-\mathbf b=[a_1-b_1,\,a_2-b_2].}$$

It is also the missing displacement in $(\mathbf a-\mathbf b)+\mathbf b=\mathbf a$.
