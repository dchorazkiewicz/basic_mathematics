Parallelism and perpendicularity from one angle comparison

Let $\mathbf u$ and $\mathbf v$ have inclination angles $\alpha$ and $\beta$. The directed turn $\beta-\alpha$ contains both geometric tests: its sine detects parallel directions and its cosine detects perpendicular directions.

Parallelism from the sine difference identity

$$\sin(\beta-\alpha)=\frac{u_1v_2-u_2v_1}{\|\mathbf u\|\,\|\mathbf v\|}.$$

For nonzero vectors the denominator is nonzero. Parallel directions occur exactly when the sine vanishes:

$$\boxed{\mathbf u\parallel\mathbf v\iff u_1v_2-u_2v_1=0.}$$

Equivalently, one vector is a nonzero scalar multiple of the other.

Perpendicularity from the cosine difference identity

$$\cos(\beta-\alpha)=\frac{u_1v_1+u_2v_2}{\|\mathbf u\|\,\|\mathbf v\|}.$$

This motivates the coordinate definition

$$\boxed{\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=u_1v_1+u_2v_2}$$

and gives the test

$$\boxed{\mathbf u\perp\mathbf v\iff\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=0.}$$
Geometric meaning of the signs

The dot product is positive for an acute angle, zero for a right angle, and negative for an obtuse angle. The quantity $u_1v_2-u_2v_1$ is zero exactly when the two directions fail to span area.
